// ATTACK CONTEXT

In real AES-128 the attacker faces a key space of 2128 possibilities. Even with the fastest conceivable hardware — performing 1018 key tests per second — a brute-force search would require approximately 1013 years to exhaust the keyspace. The observable universe is only ~1.4 × 1010 years old.

This demo succeeds because we artificially reduced the keyspace to 216 (65 536 keys) — a factor of 2112 smaller than real AES-128.
